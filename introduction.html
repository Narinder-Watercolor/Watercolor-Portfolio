I am a physicist by training, having completed my PhD in soft condensed matter physics. Currently, I work as a scientist in the Department of Physics and Astronomy at the University of Potsdam, Germany. While my professional life revolves around physics, my heart has always been that of an artist.

My passion lies in the watercolor medium, which offers a unique freedom — the fluidity of water and pigment allows the mind to wander, exploring light, transparency, and form. I have practiced watercolor for over 25 years, initially focusing on landscapes. Over time, I discovered the joy of painting human figures and faces, using colors playfully while capturing the subtle volumes of eyes, nose, lips, and ears with minimal strokes that reflect light and shadow.

Achievements

During my school years, I won several painting competitions at both state and national levels. I have conducted numerous workshops in Germany and recently participated in an exhibition in Dresden, Germany, alongside artists from Germany, the United States, Peru, Colombia, and Chile.
